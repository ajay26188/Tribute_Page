Oskar Schindler

A German industrialist, humanitarian, and member of the Nazi Party who is credited with saving the lives of 1,200 Jews during the Holocaust

Here's a time line of Oskar Schindler's life:

  • 1908 - Born in Svitavy, Moravia, Austria-Hungary
  • 1924 - Left School and started selling farm equipment for his father
  • 1928 - Married a girl name Emilie who he had met at the farm
  • 1935 - Joined the pro-Nazi Sudeten German Party
  • 1938 - Arrested by Czechoslovak authorities on charges of espionage and sentenced to Death
  • 1944 - His factory was decommissioned and he petitioned to move it to Brnenec, close to his hometowm
  • 1945 - The war in Europe ended, and the next day Schindler and his wife fled the country with the help of several of the Schindlerjuden, as the Jews he saved came to be known.
  • 1957 - Went bankrupt and the next year traveled alone to West Germany, where he made an abortive entry into the cement business.
  • 1962 - Was named a Righteous Gentile by Yad Vashem and was interred in the Catholic cemetery on Mount Zion in Jerusalem.
  • 1974 - Died in Hildesheim, West Germany
